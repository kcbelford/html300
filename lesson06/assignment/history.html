A brief history of pizza

Source: pizzafacts.net

1st Millenium BC

Archeologist in Sardinia found ancient remains of a flattened bread that was apparently very popular among the local population.

1st Century BC

Excavations were made in Pompeii under the mountain/volcano Vesuvius that proved the existence of pizzas that were common in that part of Italy. In the ruins of Pompeii archeologist even found entire untouched workshops, shops, and tools of the trade that are used for selling and production of pizzas.

1522

After tomatoes were first imported to Europe from the New World, people of Naples first managed to combine them with their bread products, creating the earliest form of modern pizza. As Naples was one of the centers of Italian food production and a busy port full of sailors, word about this new dish spread across the Europe.

17th Century

Pizza created in Naples gained great popularity among its citizens and tourists who visited that region of Italy.

1830

Antica Pizzeria Port'Alba, the first ever modern pizzeria opened in Naples.

1889

By the orders of Umberto I King of Italy and his wife Queen Margherita di Savoia, the most famous Naples pizza chef Raffaele Esposito created for them three pizzas. After the King and Queen expressed their immense satisfaction for his work, Naples became firmly the center of the pizza world, and designs of Raffaele Esposito became standard for all future pizzas. It is said that the Margherita pizza was named after Queen Margherita.

1905

First American license for baking pizzas granted to Gennaro Lombardi Spring Street in New York City ("Lombardi's Pizza").

1924

Famous American cook, and former Lombardi's pizzaiolo, Anthony "Totonno" Pero opens his Totonno's Pizzeria in Coney Island, N.Y.

1943

Ike Sewell of “Pizzeria Uno” invents Chicago-style deep-dish pizza.

1957

The first supermarket frozen pizza was released by Celentano Brothers. Soon after that, frozen pizzas became very popular across the entire US.

1958

Opening of the first Pizza Hut in Wichita, Kansas, which will later become a worldwide franchise of pizza restaurants.

1959

The first “Little Caesars” opens.

1967

The first “Domino’s” opens.

1984

The first “Papa John’s” opens.

2009

European Union established a ruling that protected Naples' Neapolitan pizza as a cultural food heritage. All pizzerias that want to create real Neapolitan pizza must comply with the strict standards for ingredients and process of its preparation.
